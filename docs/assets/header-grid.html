Why the fish aren't four colours

You asked for colour or label. I went with size + label, and I want to say why before you overrule me.

  • One accent is what makes this look expensive. Red points, cyan means alive, and nothing else competes. Four fish colours and the palette starts arguing with itself — the same reason the mouth couldn't be white and the circuits couldn't stay on the tail.
  • Size already carries seniority. A big fish reads as the architect before you've read anything. Colour would be a second, redundant channel.
  • The label removes all doubt and costs eight pixels of mono type.

If you still want role tints, the honest way is a narrow ramp within the red family — deep crimson architect through to a lighter QA — never four hues. Say the word.

What changed

  1. The tank is the right column. Label and tank now start at the same vertical position — the status line rides on top of the water rather than sitting in a row above it.
  2. No more dead space. The left column's two rows split the height, so the tabs don't float in a white gap under the brand.
  3. Fish swim beneath the label. Each one gets a lane its own body actually fits inside, so nothing collides with the text or clips the floor.
  4. Roles. Architect and BO large; dev and QA small. Each labelled. All four are agents.
  5. The bell. Recent Events as a popover with an unread badge, and a mute for ambient status. The floating panel that was covering your DONE lane is gone.
  6. Errors are not mutable. Mute silences ambient events only. "API unreachable" and "merge conflict" still surface as toasts. A toggle that can hide a broken merge is a footgun.

Still yours to call: tank height — 132px eats roughly 60px of board. And whether the bell belongs in the strip or back with the toolbar. Tell me and I'll write the component.
